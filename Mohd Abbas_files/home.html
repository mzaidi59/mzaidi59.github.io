Biography

The present is the future. Our every action changes the course of the world ever so slightly. My recently discovered aim is to find a way to make it better; and the only way I can, is through research.

I am a pre-final year undergraduate at Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur with a major in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Artificial Intelligence. I have worked on problems ranging from talking robots to energy-efficient networks and am curretly interested in medical image processing.

My broad areas of interests are: Digital Signal Processing, Probability Theory, Digital Electronics, Machine Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Computer Vision and Medical Imaging. Recently, I worked with Prof. Rajesh M Hegde on joint mobile scheduling and data aggregation in wireless sensor networks to improve the overall efficiency. I employed the use of Deep Reinforcement Learning in the venture.

Apart from my academic work, I enjoy playing and writing music. I am an experienced guitarist and my greatest satisfaction is a song well sung.

Selected Projects

The complete list is on the resume.

  • Joint Mobile Sink Scheduling and Data Aggregation in Wireless Sensor Networks using Q-Learning

    The collection of data from sensor nodes using mobile sinks becomes difficult when the network is asynchronous since the energy of the sink is also limited. To solve this problem, we implemented an algorithm that uses deep Q networks to optimize the collection of data with the mobile sink energy.

  • YantraManav: The Interactive Robot

    Conceptualized and fabricated a machine capable of interacting with the user like a human using text to speech libraries. It could also perform simple actions like checking emails and google searches upon prompting by the user.
